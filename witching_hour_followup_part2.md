# Breaking Banksy Closing Newsbreak : Voldemort’s dead aka  The Man whose name has been screamed in face for four years is really named Georgio and he wants to sell you a hat  
### A short follow-up. Public Instagram. Public website. Public press archive. The faces don't match.

---

For four years on this platform and others, people have screamed one name at me in defense of a certain artist: **Robin Gunningham**. The *Mail on Sunday* identified him in 2008. The global press has repeated it ever since. Photographs labeled "Robin Gunningham, the man behind Banksy" have been in circulation that whole time. Dark hair. Younger. A *Wanksy* hoodie in one of the more famous ones. The man you would picture if you typed the name into a search bar.

This week, on a public Instagram account called **@banksy.georgio** (1,376 followers, bio: *"Satire. Art project. Gift shop at the end."*), an older man in a Toyota-logo "TOY" cap is being publicly marketed as a Banksy-adjacent character. The account links to **banksygeorgio.com**. The character is openly promoted by another public Instagram account, **@goodguyboris** (94,800 followers), whose bio links **@thegrifters** — that's **shop.thegrifters.org**, a public storefront selling the TOY cap merchandise for €39.95. Good Guy Boris posts videos explaining the character ("TOY CAP explained"). The man in the Banksy.Giorgio videos names himself on camera. He is being promoted — also publicly — through Steve Lazarides' own social account (the *@stevelazarides* tag), which is what put this in front of me.

I'm putting two faces side by side. **The man in the press photos that have been called "Robin Gunningham" for eighteen years.** And **the man currently being publicly marketed as Banksy.Giorgio across three public commercial surfaces.** Look at the two faces. They do not match. Different age, different build, different hair, different face. Eighteen years of press identification on one set of photographs. A public commercial venture on a different man, run openly under his own face, in public, today.

I am not outing anyone. The man being marketed is naming himself in his own promotional videos, on his own Instagram, with his own face, on a public storefront. Lazarides is publicly promoting him. Good Guy Boris is publicly promoting him with explanatory video posts. The bio says *Satire. Art project. Gift shop at the end* — the *Exit Through the Gift Shop* reference is theirs, not mine. Whatever the venture is calling itself (satire, art project, parody, commercial brand — it can be all four at once and the legal category does not change the structural fact), the *face being marketed* is not the face the press has spent eighteen years calling Robin Gunningham. Same role in the public imagination. Different man.

So here is the part that is mine to say, plainly, since it has been screamed at me for four years by accounts of varying authenticity and a few real people who I think genuinely believed it:

**Rob is not Banksy. The man the press has been pointing at since 2008 is not the man currently being publicly marketed as the face of the Banksy-adjacent venture. They are not the same person. Look at the photographs.**

That's it. The cat is out of the bag this week because the venture put itself out, openly, on a public storefront with a public Instagram and a public promoter. I did not put any of that out. I am reading what is now visible to anyone with a phone.

I will say one more thing, then I am done with the name forever. The four-year campaign of yelling *Robin Gunningham* at me on this platform was not a defense of an artist. It was the maintenance of a misdirection. The misdirection is in the open now, in a gift shop, selling hats, and the inventory it was protecting got marked to market on Wednesday for $953,000 against a $1.1 million head-to-head edition-mate eighteen months earlier, in a sale the press is calling a success. Read the picture, not the caption. The picture is two different men. The caption was *Gunningham*. The hammer was $953,000. The flagship next door missed its floor by a quarter.

This is the last time the name appears under my byline. I am done with it. The four years are over. The receipts are in. Read them yourself — they are all public, they are all linked, and they all read the same way.

Exit through the gift shop. The venture's own words. Not mine.
