## Why this strand is hosted on Medium

This is the spine strand — the one that makes the others a single history instead of six parallel essays. It carries the full argument about the two rails of two-dimensional printing technology running in tension for six hundred years: the escalation rail from the goldsmith's plate through the silicon wafer to the plateless giclée print, and the craft rail of silver chlorobromide chemistry held still for a century as a position rather than a way-station.

The full text — Blake using the plate backwards, Michael A. Smith running the craft rail intact through the brand-architecture age, Brett Weston burning the negatives on his eightieth birthday, Damien Hirst's announced two-hundred-year posthumous-production machine, and the giclée terminus that ends the escalation rail with no plate at all — is at the link above.
