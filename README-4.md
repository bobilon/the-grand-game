## What This Is

This is a working cultural-studies document. It integrates art into a synthetic history of roughly the last two hundred and fifty years, with the through-lines kept contained — held to the minimum needed — to inform my last Reddit post on the Banksy question. It is the last I will have to say on that subject under this byline until I publish a separate work documenting the totality of the experience and what it taught me. That later work will be informed by these working documents, for anyone who has an interest in tracing how the argument was assembled.

The seven strands here were not written as reading material for a general audience. They were written as the load-bearing structure under a specific public argument, and they have the texture of working notes that hold together as one argument. I am putting them in one bound document so that anyone who wants to follow the through-lines from the public posts can do so, and so that the work exists in a stable form for the later integrated history to draw on.

I am not big on information capture. This document is open. No paywall, no tracking, no ask. If it is useful, take from it; if it is not, leave it. The work was done for its own reasons. Putting it on the record is the last thing the project required.

A note on scope: the documentary apparatus the work rests on — UK Companies House filings, Jersey Financial Services Commission filings, named auction-house catalog records with lot numbers and dates, named books with authors and ISBNs, public-domain literary sources, and the dated press articles — is what carries the structural claims. Where a specific claim about a living named individual would require evidence that is not publicly verifiable, the work stays at the structural level and does not name. That wall is held throughout. The compass behind it stays in the channel that can subpoena records, not in the public text.

## Reading order

The strands are numbered in the order they were written to hand off to one another, but each is self-contained. If you want the spine first, read Industry (Strand II, on Medium). If you want the tool first, read Law and Fiction (Strand I). If you want the philosophical close, read the Grand Game capstone (Strand VII). If you came here from one of the Banksy posts, start with the Appendices.
